Leadership and Management

Overview

Leadership and Management course aims to equip participants to effectively lead and manage health organizations within the context of Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). This course will enhance leadership, managerial, interpersonal, political, and systems thinking skills desired to successfully lead an agency in a changing global environment while meeting the demands of keeping the world safe. The interconnectedness of our global health village requires health leaders to be well-rounded having the right skills and competencies mix to lead and manage national agencies for early warning, risk reduction and management of national and global public health threats. This course is specifically designed for executive level leaders and managers currently leading the NPHIs or other agencies with similar roles. The course combines theory with practice through lectures, relevant literature, written assignments, group exercises and case studies as well as sharing experiences from speakers who are successfully leading or led public health agencies in developing countries. Assessment of course participants in leadership and management will be accomplished through individual and team exercises.

Learning Objectives

  • Apply skills and behaviors associated with effective leadership and management
  • Evaluate the leadership and management role within the organizational setting

  • Develop strategies to delegate tasks and supervise team members effectively and efficiently
  • Develop strategy for creating and managing change in the organization
  • Explain the importance and methods of using data for decision-making

Instructors

Wondimagegnehu Alemu, MD, MPH&TM, FAIPH

Dr. Alemu is a Medical Doctor & public health specialist with vast experience in global health leadership and management, health security and health emergency preparedness and response, health systems strengthening, disease surveillance, prevention and control. He has held leadership and management positions at national and international levels. Before, retiring from International civil service, he was Head of WHO Country Office in Nigeria leading & guiding the organization’s normative work of advising Government on health matters. He also led WHO’s direct health intervention to North East Humanitarian Crisis and Polio Eradication Initiative. Dr Alemu also served as WHO Representative in Uganda & Sierra Leone and as a medical officer at WHO Regional Office for Africa led & coordinated the development and implementation of Integrated Disease Surveillance in the region. Before joining WHO, he worked for UNICEF as a National health project officer. He also held various positions in his native country including Regional Health manager, Head of National Immunization Programme and Head of Epidemiological Surveillance Division. During his 35 years of services, he has worked extensively with governments, Non-Governmental Organizations, CSOs and intergovernmental and bilateral organization in Global Health. He is champion and strong advocate for community engagement in community health, #health equity, #gender equality and empowerment; #UHC and #Health security.
