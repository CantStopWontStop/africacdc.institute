Introduction to Antimicrobial Resistance

The primary audience for this course is policy makers and managers working in public health. The objective of this course is to increase the audience’s awareness and understanding about antimicrobial resistance (AMR) among human and animals and interventions to control it at the global, regional, national, and facility level.

Introduction to Antimicrobial Resistance introduces participants to 1) The epidemiology of AMR and its global impact, and 2) Strategies to improve surveillance of AMR organisms among humans and animals; delay emergence of AMR ; limit transmission of AMR; and mitigate harm among patients infected with AMR organisms . This course will equip policy decision makers and health service managers with the evidence base and public health principles and approaches to inform policies and regulations to facilitate implementation of standard practices to combat AMR.

Learning Objectives

  • Improve awareness on how AMR emerges and spreads
  • Describe the burden of AMR across healthcare, agriculture, and the environment
  • Assess the impact of AMR on the health systems and society
  • Describe strategies and interventions to contain AMR
  • Improve understanding of global guidelines for AMR control in human and animals
  • Understand the importance of AMR diagnostics, surveillance and partnership in control efforts
  • Develop multidisciplinary approaches to implement prevention and spread of AMR across human and animal health (One health)
  • Understand Infection prevention and control in health care facilities
  • Strengthen capacity to describe gaps and design relevant policies and regulations for national AMR
  • Understand the need for investment in novel strategies, interventions and products to contain
