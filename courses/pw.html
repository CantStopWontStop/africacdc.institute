Proposal Writing

Overview

This course covers the mechanics of grant writing, including both scientific and administrative components. It uses didactic and applied training to build competency in developing a proposal for a public health program or research project. The course walks trainees through a step-by-step approach to transform their ideas for public health programs/research projects into proposals ready for submission to a funding agency. In tandem, it equips trainees with the skills to identify a suitable funding opportunity, distinguishing between different types of funding mechanisms and funding agencies/ donors, and fitting their ideas to the funding agency’s guidelines.

Course offered Fall 2019

7-week online training | 3-days in-person workshop

This course is designed for personnel from Africa CDC, National Public Health Institutes, Ministries of Health, and academic institutions involed in the process of developing proposals.

Approx. 8-week course | Suggested 6 hours/week

English

Apply

Learning Objectives

  • Identify a priority area for a public health program/research project
  • Formulate an objective and specific aims for the public health program/research project
  • Write a targeted review of the literature to relate program/research aims to the current state of knowledge/ practice
  • Determine appropriate methods for:
    1. program/research design;
    2. target audience selection and sampling;
    3. data collection;
    4. and data management
  • Develop a budget and budget justification for the program/research project
  • Develop a timeline for program implementation
  • Develop a monitoring and evaluation plan
  • Identify different types of funding mechanisms and funding agencies
  • Develop written and oral scientific communication skills
