Proposal Development for Public Health Research and Programs

Overview

This course covers the mechanics of grant writing, including both scientific and administrative components. It uses didactic and applied training to build competency in developing a proposal for a public health program or research project. The course walks trainees through a step-by-step approach to transform their ideas for public health programs or research projects into proposals ready for submission to a funding agency. In tandem, it equips trainees with the skills to identify a suitable funding opportunity, distinguishing between different types of funding mechanisms and funding agencies, to fit their ideas to the funding agencyâ€™s guidelines, and to navigate the grant review process.

 

*Priority in course admission will be granted to applicants who

  1. are in the process of developing a proposal for a research project or public health program or
  2. have a proposal idea that they intend to develop and submit for funding in the near future.

Course offered Sep - Nov, 2019 | Deadline for applications has closed.

Approx. 8-week course | Suggested 6 hours/week

7-week online training | 3-day in-person workshop for select participants

The primary target audience for this course is:

  • Public health professional within Africa CDC, National Public Health Institutes (NPHI), and the Ministries of Health who are involved in the process of developing proposals.
  • Members of academic institutions and Africa CDC- or NPHI-partner agencies who are involved in the process of developing proposals.

Learning Objectives

By the end of the training, trainees are expected to achieve the following learning objectives:

  • Describe the technical and administrative components of a proposal
  • Identify a priority area for a public health program or research project
  • Identify different types of funding mechanisms
  • Find a funding mechanism that is a good fit for your idea
  • Identify different types of funding agencies
  • Find a funding agency that is a good fit for your idea
  • Describe the importance of following funding agency guidelines
  • Identify and engage relevant stakeholders
  • Develop a proposal development plan
  • Develop well-formulated objectives or research questions for the public health program or research project
  • Retrieve scientific evidence from a variety of sources
  • Identify principles of referencing and in-text citation
  • Use reference management tools (e.g. EndNote Web, etc.)
  • Write a targeted review of the literature to relate program or research aims to the current state of knowledge and/ or practice
  • Develop the rationale for the proposed work
  • Develop a significance statement for the work
  • Plan methods for a feasible and credible program or research study
  • Identify the principles of ethical public health practice and research conduct
  • Apply awareness of cultural values to the design of public health programs
  • Identify the expected short- and long-term outcomes of the work
  • Develop a timeline for program implementation
  • Develop a dissemination plan
  • Develop a sustainability plan
  • Develop a monitoring and evaluation plan
  • Develop a succinct and thorough abstract for the proposed work
  • Develop a budget and budget justification for the program or research project
  • Develop key personnel qualifications statement
  • Develop letters of support
  • Develop an institutional capacity statement
  • Identify key aspects of the proposal submission process
  • Identify key features of the proposal review process
  • Identify best practices for responding to reviewers and resubmitting proposal
  • Develop written and oral scientific communication skills
  • Perform peer review
