Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention

The Africa Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (Africa CDC) supports African Union Member States to improve the health of their people by reducing disease burdens - especially infectious diseases. One way is through building public health workforce capacity. The Africa CDC Institute for Workforce Development (IWD) is integral to this goal and aims to build workforce capacity to prevent, detect, and respond to all health threats and emergencies (including epidemics, man-made and natural disasters, and public health events of international concern).

Courses

Transforming Public Health Surveillance

Transforming Public Health Surveillance (TPHS) provides a thorough overview of the history, purposes, activities, uses, elements, data sources, models, analyses, actions, preparation of reports, evaluation, and ethical and legal issues of public health surveillance (PHS).


View Course

Introduction to Antimicrobial Resistance

The course provides an overview of the history, risk and burden of antimicrobial resistance (AMR), implications of use and misuse of antimicrobials in humans, animals and the environment, AMR diagnostics, surveillance and strategies including the one health approach to contain AMR.


View Course

Proposal Writing

The course provides guidance on developing a strong application that will allow reviewers to better evaluate the science and merit of the proposal. Participants will learn how to put their ideas into proper words, arrange a clear and concise paper and search grant market for potential funders.

Leadership and Management

Leadership and Management course aims to equip participants to effectively lead and manage health organizations within the context of Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). This course will enhance leadership, managerial, interpersonal, political, and systems thinking skills desired to successfully lead an agency in a changing global environment while meeting the demands of keeping the world safe.
